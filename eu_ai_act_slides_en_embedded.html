The context

The clock is ticking for every industry

The EU AI Act classifies as high-risk any AI system that impacts fundamental rights: HR, credit, insurance, healthcare, education, law enforcement and more. Non-compliance carries fines up to €15M or 3% of global turnover.

⚠️
High-risk enforcement date: August 2, 2026 (possible deferral to August 2027)
Feb 2025
Prohibited AI practices ban in effect
Aug 2025
General-purpose AI (GPAI) obligations
Aug 2026
Full high-risk AI requirements
Aug 2027
AI in regulated products (MDR, machinery)

Regulatory precedents

GDPR fines: a preview of the EU AI Act

European authorities are already sanctioning AI practices under GDPR. The EU AI Act will add AI-specific obligations with its own fines.

€1,200M
Meta (Facebook)
EU→US data transfer without adequate safeguards. Direct risk for models sending data to external APIs.
€345M
TikTok
Processing of minors' data and lack of transparency in algorithmic decisions.
€310M
LinkedIn
Behavioral profiling for targeted advertising. Analogous to behavior-based scoring in any sector.
€40M
Criteo
Tracking without valid consent. Precedent for models using data without clear legal basis.
€30.5M
Clearview AI
Massive scraping of biometric data without consent. Category 1 of Annex III.
€15M
OpenAI (ChatGPT)
Lack of transparency and insufficient legal basis. First direct precedent against an LLM provider.
